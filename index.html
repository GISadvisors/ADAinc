Our Project

The Municipality of Port Hope currently houses thousands of water and wastewater service records in physical form. Managing physical documents is costly, inefficient and makes it difficult to track inventory. To improve their asset management practices and be able to locate utility infrastructure in the field, the Municipality desired to have their service lines and valves mapped as accurately as possible.

Over the course of the project, three solutions to map data were explored: automation to plot points, manually mapping points, and utilizing a mobile data collection application. The amount of time to use and accuracy were compared for each solution and Port Hope's existing geodatabases were updated using the results.

Automation

Using ArcGIS Pro, Python code blocks and SQL queries, a series of automated workflows were created to accelerate the mapping process of valves and service lines.

Comparing Mapping Methods

AUTOMATION- Custom tool to map valves at parcel centroids
Time per valve: N/A
Total Time: ~1 minute
Uncertainty: Solution doesn't account for building locations
Overall Rank:  4th
AUTOMATION- Custom tool mapping valves using building offsets
Time per valve: 30 seconds
Total Time: 42 hours/1 week
Uncertainty: Positional accuracy of OSM building footprints
Overall Rank:  3rd
MANUAL- Mapping valve locations by hand
Time per valve: 2 minutes
Total Time: 167 hours/4.5 weeks
Uncertainty: Human error/distinguishing aerial imagery
Overall Rank:  2nd
MOBILE- ArcGIS Field Maps data collection
Time per valve: 3.5 minutes
Total Time: 438 hours/12 weeks
Uncertainty: +/- 5 meters; Quality of GNSS
Overall Rank:  1st
